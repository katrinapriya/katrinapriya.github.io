Background

My team and I were passionate about facilitating collective impact for social causes. We envisioned an app that would enable anyone to be a changemaker in their community. We grew our idea through pitch competitions, startup incubators, mentorship, and more. Our project was a finalist for the global Big Ideas Social Innovation Contest.


Research & Ideation

Initially, we weren't sure who our core users would be. We wanted to learn more about the pain points of various groups to explore ways to facilitate collective social impact. We conducted over 25 in-depth interviews and received 100+ survey responses from students, professionals, companies, nonprofits, and more.

Here are some of the insights we gained from the interviews and surveys.

The problem we saw is a disconnect between motivated and skilled individuals (students and professionals) and existing, under-resourced opportunities for social good (nonprofits, social initiatives).

After discussing the insights from our interviews and surveys, we diverged to 2 different concepts.

Concept 1: Small to medium-sized companies have trouble identifying causes that their employees care about, while professionals wish that they could make a greater social impact. We could connect employees with impactful opportunities that align with their interests and skills, and send analytics to their company.

Concept 2: We create a platform for students to browse through skill-based opportunities of interest. There could be an educational component that could include changemaking techniques, since we identified that many students don't know how to turn their ideas into action.

We researched both ideas a bit more, and went back-and-forth between the two several times. Eventually, we went with concept 2 because it aligned with our unified vision that anyone can be a changemaker in their community.


Solution

We wanted our solution to contain 3 features to alleviate students' frustrations that were identified in surveying and interviewing.

  • Ability to join and start social initiatives users care about
  • Education on social issues and changemaking techniques
  • User-to-user interaction to foster a community of changemakers

We performed a competitive analysis to illustrate our unique features.


With this in mind, we began sketching and wireframing. We showed our low-fi prototypes to students and mentors to continue iterating on them.


I created this simple hi-fidelity prototype of our envisioned app. You can interact with the prototype here.


When signing up, users are asked to indicate social causes they care about and their skills. Once on the app, they can explore educational content, impactful tasks posted by individuals & organizations, local events, and ideas for social initiatives.
