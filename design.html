Trackin' Tag

Spring 2019, Product Design

CHALLENGE
According to the Department of Transportation, airlines loose 10% of luggage. Losing luggage not only leads to a bad experience for passengers, but it also costs airline company $900 million a year for insurance. Almost every person we interviewed had a story of their bags being lost or delayed.

CONTEXT
I worked on this idea for my product design class in a team of 6 students primarily from business backgrounds. We developed this product throughout the semester and conducted a lot of user research. At the end of the semester, our product was featured at a showcase at Jacobs Hall.

RESEARCH
From our interviews, we decided that our target customer is a business traveler.



Here is our business model canvas.



OUR SOLUTION
We designed an electronic bag tag gets rid of those long sticky paper tags we print at airports for checked-in luggage. The tag would contain an RFID chip so that it can be tracked after the flight. This would reduce lost luggage at airports. We also hope to implement an alert on a phone app for when the bag arrives at baggage claim. I developed the mockups of the app interface, as well as the logo.





We used 3D printing to create this final mockup of our product!
