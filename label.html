Background

Smart home devices are popular devices ranging from smart thermostats and speakers to voice-enabled assistants such as Amazon Echo and Google Home. Unfortunately, the majority of these devices don't have privacy and security by design. My team and I wanted to bring security, privacy, and transparency to the smart home device experience.


Research & Analysis

We conducted a total of 16 interviews with smart home device users, experts, and stakeholders. Our interviews illustrated a lack of urgency for strong security and privacy measures from users, who often undervalue their data. This lack of urgency also exists in device makers, as they are not being held accountable for their poor security standards.

We used affinity diagramming to extract common themes from our interviews.


Smart home device users were instead focused on convenience and accessibility. In order to get users to care about cybersecurity, we reframed our project to solve the following question: How might we make security and privacy accessible and essential to smart home device consumers?


Ideation

We brainstormed solutions and created clusters of common themes. We then converged to 3 solutions.


After discussing our ideas, we felt that redesigning a smart home device could make the device less convenient and usable as we replace features. Meanwhile, our second option was dependent on the financial capabilities of smart home device companies and users, which reduces its accessibility.

We chose to pursue the privacy & security label, as it provides the most accessibility to consumers, while also making security more essential for the device makers.


Prototyping

To make the label's content relevant, we researched aspects of privacy and security that should concern users. We created the initial prototypes after making the following design choices:

  • We used a table layout to keep the information organized and easy-to-read. In terms of design, we were inspired by the Nutrition Facts label on food boxes today.


  • We added a QR code that links to a webpage explaining concepts from the label that might be unknown to the average consumer. We each came up with our own wireframes for the initial design.


  • We included face icons to conveniently illustrate level of caution to users with less knowledge of security. We consulted with a red-green color blind group member to choose accessible shades of red, yellow, and green.


User Testing

We each showed our prototypes to several smart home device users. I incorporated their feedback to make the following design changes:

  • I expanded the face icons to every row on the label, instead of just the security section. Users found the emojis extremely useful and wanted it for the privacy section, so I made sure to utilize it throughout the sticker.

  • I made the label smaller. Once I had the physical label prototype in my hands, I realized that it was too big for smaller device boxes. Users wanted all of the information to stay on the label, so I had to alter the design to fit it all.

  • I made the webpage more user-friendly. Drop down menus and large chunks of text were not accessible to users. I designed the layout to be similar to a phone's Settings page, so that it feels familiar to users.


Final Design

Through the insights gained from usability testing of our designs, I created our final prototype.


I kept the webpage clean and monochromatic to match the label's design. You can interact with our webpage prototype, view our Behance page, and enjoy our YouTube video showing the label in use!


Takeaways

  • It's very important to keep security and privacy in mind while designing products.
  • It's not enough to just inform users about important issues like cybersecurity, you have to also get them to care.
  • Empathy goes a long way in interviewing and understanding users.
  • Trust your teammates and be flexible when things don't go your way.
  • Talking to experts in a field you don't know much about can be intimidating. By researching the topics beforehand, you can be prepared to make the most out of the interview!
