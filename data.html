    Introducing a data-first approach to building apps

    CONTEXT
    UI/UX for building with data schemas for no-code software startup, Unqork
    ROLE & DURATION
    Lead product designer;
    9 months
    METHODS & TOOLS
    User research, diagramming, product definition & strategy, usability tests, Figma


    I joined Unqork's Data Experience team as their first designer. Through rounds of product definition, research, and design iterations, I crafted a data-first approach that allows users to define their data schemas upfront, and seamlessly integrate those fields into their application.


    Problem: Users wanting to build with a data-first approach, the ideal standard for app development, face a tedious, error-prone, and manual process in Unqork.

    Solution: Introduce a guided experience for integrating data schemas to a user's workflow, while promoting best practices and data integrity.



    DEFINING THE SPACE

    I crafted visual & written artifacts to drive stakeholder alignment, and thereby inform product strategy and requirements.


    As the team’s first product designer, I helped transition a backend-focused team into a more user-facing product team. There was limited product management support, with engineers often driving requirements.

    To understand the highly technical and ambiguous data modeling space, I conducted 1-on-1 sessions with each team member. I felt like a detective, piecing together information from these sessions and consolidating project-related resources.

    Visual artifacts were instrumental in clarifying relationships & terminology, resolving internal confusion and misalignment.


    Ultimately, these efforts resulted in a documented "single source of truth" that not only guided our project, but also served as a valuable resource for onboarding new team members to the project.





    EXTRACTING USER INSIGHTS

    I conducted user research to understand the pain points of building with data schemas in Unqork today, and gather feedback on initial design explorations.


    Current flow of building with schemas based on user interviews


    Here were some of the user pain points:

    1. Schema fields are being defined in hacky, unintuitive ways since there is no dedicated schema builder interface.
    2. Updating modules that use schemas is tedious and prone to errors. It is hard to track dependencies or changes.
    3. Creators are using hidden components without validations, resulting in unclear & missing data types, validations, and nested data.

    I shared two different visualization methods for schemas. The Nested View better met user expectations, feeling simpler to read and more familiar. The ERD View was confusing, likely because users expected to see relationships between schemas in a diagram format, not the schema itself.






    FINAL DESIGNS

    Using Unqork's design system, I iterated on designs and handed off a schema builder interface, alongside designs for data models & record collections.



    Drag-and-drop visual schema builder, allowing users to clearly define their data fields upfront



    Ability to add field validations, ensuring clear and accurate data types & rules



    Guided experience for understanding where the schema fits in the overall user flow



    Seamlessly bring in data fields into modules, ensuring data integrity throughout the app




    RESULTS

    These features successfully introduced a data-first approach to building in Unqork that was well-received by users and stakeholders.


    Our initial release enables users to import data schemas and integrate those data fields into application modules. While it's too early to collect quantitative user data on its impact, here is some qualitative feedback:
