    • Logic Vision

    Slashing time-to-build in no-code with a unified, visual logic canvas

    CONTEXT
    Design-led project to craft the vision state of Unqork's logic building experience
    ROLE & DURATION
    Lead product designer;
    6+ months
    METHODS & TOOLS
    User research, competitive analysis, diagramming, wireframing, workshops, Figma


    Over the past several months, I've been defining the vision for configuring logic in Unqork, the core feature of our no-code platform. Our users spend up to 75% of their time building logic, but one has to be a trained (6+ months) configurator to make sense of it.


    Problem: Currently, building logic in Unqork is perplexing to understand or orchestrate at scale.

    Solution: A powerful & easy-to-use logic canvas focused on reusability, real-time debugging, and smart suggestions.



    DISCOVERY & RESEARCH

    Through discovery research & user interviews, I uncovered current challenges and visual approaches to no-code logic building.


    Users build logic in Unqork today via 5 types of logic components


    From my research, here were some of the challenges of building logic in Unqork:

    1. Understanding Complexity: Configured logic can be challenging to grasp, causing confusion among team members. UI is also tangled with logic on the canvas.
    2. Lack of Reusability: There is no way to save, organize, and share logic, leading to redundant and inconsistent logic across modules.
    3. No Guardrails: The lack of guardrails in our logic components makes it easy to build something confusing quickly.

    I also conducted a competitive analysis to understand how other low/no-code platforms approach logic, with a focus on their visualization methods and key features.






    ALIGNMENT & DEFINITION

    I facilitated stakeholder alignment through workshops and evolving artifacts, establishing the product & design strategy.


    The problem space is vast and complex; and with evolving stakeholders, this remains a design-led project. I facilitated 3 workshops (virtual and in-person) to gather stakeholders and align on key project elements.

    Together, we defined project objectives, terminology, use-cases, and personas. I crafted artifacts based on our conversations, which continue to evolve.







    Our project objectives were to 1) establish a powerful framework that meets user needs, 2) empower creators to configure & test logic confidently, and 3) facilitate teams to configure logic at scale, while maintaining quality.




    DESIGN EXPLORATIONS

    I explored design patterns to deconstruct complex logic, iterating based on stakeholder and user feedback.


    The most difficult problem to tackle is how to visualize and understand complex logic in the no-code world. In an Unqork module, there may be thousands of logic elements with deep nesting. Here are some of the design concepts I’ve been exploring to address this:


    1. Ability to view logic at different granularity levels. These views enable users to focus on one aspect at a time, avoiding cognitive overload.


      High-level view of functions & execution order; helps scale logic via modularization



      View inside of a function; tackles complex, nested logic by obscuring details of operations



      View of operations in a control flow; provides a focused look at operations and their execution order



    2. Reusability as the standard. Encourage reusable functions that can be defined at the workspace or individual module level.


      Ability to drag in workspace-level functions to the module-level logic


    3. Visibility into which execution path you’re on. For debugging or testing, it’s important to know where in the flow you are and where you are going.


      Use the navigation tool to see predecessors, children, or neighbors of a piece of logic


    NEXT STEPS

    I am continuing to explore and user test these concepts before delivering an end-to-end prototype.
