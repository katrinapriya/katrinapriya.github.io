Ball of Fortune

August 2014, Team Treehouse

During my freshman year of high school, the app Flappy Bird was extremely popular. I was inspired by the success of such a simple app to create my own apps. Over the summer, I read Android app development books and eventually went through an online Android course by Team Treehouse. Through this course, I was able to create this simple fortune telling app.


WHAT I LEARNED

I learned how to develop an Android app from start to finish. I became familiar with using Eclipse and began to understand how to write the code for linking an action, like shaking the phone, to a certain part of the code. I also figured out how to publish apps to the Google Play Store.

Add One

June 2017, LearnAppMaking

I was fascinated by how structured and organized Xcode was, and felt that it would be fun to create an iOS app on it. I looked to the company LearnAppMaking for guidance. I attended an Apple iOS workshop over the summer, where I learned basic Swift. I used the graphics the website provided and made this app using my own Swift knowledge and LearnAppMaking resources. In this game, you add one to every number. You have one minute to do as many as possible.


WHAT I LEARNED

I learned how to apply Swift to a game with a timer, a score keeper, and cute graphics. It was interesting to figure out how to make sure the app looks good on all devices and in different orientations. This gave me the confidence to make my own apps from scratch, since I was able to go through the entire iOS app developing process.

DomainateTheWorld

April 2017, AthenaHacks

Due to other time-conflicting commitments, I was only able to attend USC's AthenaHacks for a few hours. In those few hours, I created a domain with my partner, Lauren Yoo, in order to try to win an award from Domain.com. We actually did end up winning the "Best Domain Name" category. Our domain name was DomainateTheWorld!


WHAT I LEARNED

I had no prior experience to creating domains, so this was an exciting task. I learned all about the creation of websites and how websites are managed. I also learned to attend entire hackathons, instead of just being there for a couple of hours!
